Human aquaporin 4.

PDB ID: 3GD8
Uniprot ID: P55087 (AQP4_HUMAN)
Function: Forms a water-specific channel. Plays an important role in brain water homeostasis and in glymphatic solute transport (is a functional waste clearance pathway for the vertebrate central nervous system ). Required for a normal rate of water exchange across the blood brain interface. Required for normal levels of cerebrospinal fluid influx into the brain cortex and parenchyma along paravascular spaces that surround penetrating arteries, and for normal drainage of interstitial fluid along paravenous drainage pathways. Thereby, it is required for normal clearance of solutes from the brain interstitial fluid, including soluble beta-amyloid peptides derived from APP. Plays a redundant role in urinary water homeostasis and urinary concentrating ability . subcelluar location: plasma membrane, endosome

Disulphide bonds.

No disulfide bonds were detected in the protein. There are only six cysteines in the protein, they are located at a great distance from each other. Minimal distanse between two cystein's sulfur is 12.86 Å. In the figure, cysteines are colored in orange.

Salt bridges
A salt bridge is a connection between a positively and a negatively charged particles whose length does not exceed 4A. Amino acids can interact in this way in proteins which are protonated (lysine, arginine, histolysis) and deprotonated (silenced, aspartate). In Aquaporin 4, 4 pairs of amino acids interact in this way (shown in table 1). The ‘measure RANDOME ALL' command and this service were used to search for amino acids.

Hydrophobic clusters
This protein is transmembrane so it has hydrophobic clusters. A hydrophobic cluster is a group of amino acids that enter into hydrophobic interactions with each other (not necessarily all with all). the ‘measure RANGE ALL ' method and the same Tull method were used to detect such clusters. As a result, 15 such clusters were found.
Analysis of residues' surroundings.

After reviewing the hydrophobic cores, several residues were selected that meet the requirements of the task. Environment of that residues was analyzed. Based on the actions shown in the applet, it can be concluded that the atoms completely covering the surface of the residue are located at a distance of about 5 angstroms from it. Quite a lot of surrounding atoms heave appeared at a distance of 4 angstroms, as can be seen in the drawings, but we can talk about the complete coverage of the residue at a distance of 5 angstroms. The table above was compiled to calculate the characteristic distance between the atoms in the nucleus. It provides distance measurements for nearby atoms in a hydrophobic cluster. The distances were calculated both for the residues located at a large distance in the chain from each other, and at a small distance. The average distance is 4.27 Å. Based on the average values for the water atom - just over 1.4 angstroms, after subtracting the maximum possible sum of the radii of the molecules, equal to 3.7(the distance between two carbons), it remains more than 0.57 Å for the average value. There's no room for water in that space. But if we take the maximum of the values given in the table-6.74, there remains a distance equal to 3.04 angstroms. It can fit a water molecule with a diameter of just over 2.8 Å.

Interaction with the ligand

During the analysis of hydrophobic interactions of our protein revealed only one hydrophobic interaction with a ligand (betaoctilglycoside) via tyrosine-231.

Hydrogen and ionic bonds between ligands and protein could not be detected, although it is possible to assume their presence. This can be most likely explained by insufficient model resolution.

Position in the membrane..

Human aquaporin 4 passes through the membrane 6 times. There is a TMHMM program for rapid analysis of protein transmembrane helices based on probabilistic models . We compare here it’s results with the results of heavier programs that estimate the conformation of a protein with minimal energy.

We can see that the accuracy of TMHMM is actually less than in previous measurements. We can suggest that this the difference is detected partly due to the fact that TMHMM does not take into account tetramericity.

However, this did not prevent to predict the pore in aquaporin based on TMHMM results, so it is suitable for initial analysis .
